## 1. Employment Disputes

### 1.1 ET1 - Employment Tribunal Claim Form

**Official Form Name:** Make a claim to an employment tribunal: Form ET1  
**Form ID:** ET1  
**Last Updated:** 25 March 2024  
**Official URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/make-a-claim-to-an-employment-tribunal-form-et1

**What it's for:**  
To make a claim to an employment tribunal if you believe you've been treated unfairly by your employer, potential employer, or trade union. Can be submitted online or via paper form.

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Section 1: Your details (name, address, contact)
- Section 2: ACAS Early Conciliation certificate number (mandatory)
- Section 3: Respondent details (employer information)
- Section 4: Type of claim (unfair dismissal, discrimination, etc.)
- Section 5: Details of claim (statement of facts)
- Section 6: Remedy sought (compensation, reinstatement, etc.)
- Section 7: Additional information
- Section 8: Signature and date

**Prerequisites:**
- **ACAS Early Conciliation certificate** (mandatory - see section 1.2)
- Must notify ACAS within 3 months minus 1 day from when the problem occurred
- Some claims exempt (e.g., interim relief in unfair dismissal cases)

**Time Limits:**
- **3 months minus 1 day** from the date of the incident/termination
- Time limit extended by ACAS early conciliation period
- Must have at least 1 month remaining after receiving ACAS certificate

**Filing Location:**
- Online: Employment Tribunal portal (preferred)
- Post: Employment Tribunal Central Office
- Email: Available for some submissions

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Missing ACAS certificate number
- Missing the 3-month deadline
- Insufficient detail in statement of facts
- Not specifying all types of claim
- Incorrect respondent details

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Medium complexity
- 4-8 pages typically
- Can be lengthy if multiple claims or complex facts

### 1.3 ACAS Early Conciliation Notification

**Official Form Name:** ACAS Early Conciliation Notification  
**Form ID:** Online form (no paper form number)  
**Official URL:** https://www.acas.org.uk/notify/start

**What it's for:**  
Mandatory first step before making an employment tribunal claim. ACAS attempts to resolve disputes through free conciliation.

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Personal details
- Employer details
- Description of dispute
- Preferred contact method
- Whether you want ACAS to contact employer

**Prerequisites:**
- None - available to all employees/workers

**Time Limits:**
- Must notify ACAS within **3 months minus 1 day** from incident
- Early conciliation can last up to **12 weeks** (as of December 2025)
- Previously 6 weeks for notifications before December 2025

**Filing Location:**
- Online only: acas.org.uk/notify/start
- Phone: 0300 123 1122

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Waiting too long before notifying ACAS
- Not providing accurate employer details
- Not engaging with conciliation process

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Low complexity
- Online form takes 10-15 minutes
- Simple questionnaire format

### 1.4 Schedule of Loss

**Official Form Name:** Schedule of Loss (No official form - template/document)  
**Form ID:** N/A (custom document)

**What it's for:**  
Sets out financial losses suffered as a result of unfair dismissal or other employment claims. Submitted before final hearing.

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Case details (tribunal name, case number, parties)
- Key information:
  - Gross annual pay
  - Net weekly pay
  - Pension contributions
  - Other benefits
  - Notice period
  - Age and length of service
- **Basic Award** calculation:
  - Based on age, length of service, weekly pay
  - Government calculator available
- **Compensatory Award** calculation:
  - Past losses (from dismissal to hearing)
  - Future losses (beyond hearing)
  - Loss of statutory rights
  - Injury to feelings (lower/middle/upper bands)
- Supporting evidence references

**Prerequisites:**
- Active employment tribunal claim
- Evidence of losses (payslips, receipts, etc.)

**Time Limits:**
- Usually required before final hearing
- Check tribunal directions for specific deadline

**Filing Location:**
- Submitted to tribunal and served on respondent
- Usually by email or post

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Inflated figures without justification
- Missing categories of loss
- Not providing supporting evidence
- Incorrect basic award calculation
- Unrealistic future loss projections

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Medium-high complexity
- 5-15 pages typically
- Requires financial calculations and evidence gathering

**Resources:**
- Citizens Advice guidance available
- Government basic award calculator
- Template available from various legal sources

## 2. County Court Claims

### 2.1 N1 - Claim Form (CPR Part 7)

**Official Form Name:** Make a claim against a person or organisation – claim form (CPR Part 7): Form N1  
**Form ID:** N1  
**Last Updated:** 8 January 2025  
**Official URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/form-n1-claim-form-cpr-part-7

**What it's for:**  
Standard form to make a claim against a person or organisation under CPR Part 7 to settle a dispute (money claims, contract disputes, etc.)

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Claimant details
- Defendant details
- Brief details of claim
- **Particulars of Claim** (can be on form or attached separately):
  - Statement of facts
  - Legal basis
  - Amount claimed
  - Interest calculation
- Value of claim
- Court fee calculation
- Statement of truth
- Signature

**Prerequisites:**
- Should send Letter Before Claim first (Pre-Action Protocol)
- Must have valid cause of action
- Court fee required (varies by claim value)

**Time Limits:**
- **6 years** for most contract/tort claims (limitation period)
- **3 years** for personal injury
- Check specific limitation periods for your claim type

**Filing Location:**
- Online: Money Claims Online (for claims under £100,000)
- Post: County Court Money Claims Centre
- In person: Local County Court

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Missing limitation deadline
- Insufficient particulars of claim
- Incorrect defendant details
- Wrong court fee
- Not serving properly

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Medium complexity
- 2-4 pages (form) + particulars of claim (varies)
- Can be complex for multi-track claims

**Related Forms:**
- N1A: Notes for claimants
- N1C: Guidance for defendants
- N1D: Out of jurisdiction guidance
- N1(FD): Consumer Credit Act guidance

**Official Form Name:** N/A (Online process)  
**Form ID:** N1C (guidance document)

**What it's for:**  
Guidance for defendants replying to claim form. Small claims are typically under £10,000.

**Key Information:**
- Defendants have 14 days to respond if particulars included
- If "particulars to follow" noted, wait for particulars (within 14 days)
- Can respond by: paying, admitting with time to pay, or disputing

**Time Limits:**
- **14 days** from service of claim form (if particulars included)
- **14 days** from service of particulars (if served separately)

**Filing Location:**
- Online: Money Claims Online
- Post: Address on claim form

## 3. Housing & Tenancy

### 3.1 Section 21 Notice (Form 6A)

**Official Form Name:** Form 6A - Notice seeking possession of a property let on an assured shorthold tenancy  
**Form ID:** Form 6A  
**Official URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/form-6a-notice-seeking-possession-of-a-property-let-on-an-assured-shorthold-tenancy

**What it's for:**  
Landlord's notice to evict tenants with assured shorthold tenancies after fixed term ends or during periodic tenancy. "No-fault" eviction.

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Landlord details
- Tenant details
- Property address
- Date notice served
- Date tenant must leave (minimum 2 months)
- Information about deposit protection
- Information about tenant's rights

**Prerequisites:**
- Assured shorthold tenancy
- At least 4 months since tenancy started
- Deposit protected in scheme
- Required documents provided (EPC, 'How to Rent' guide, gas safety certificate)
- No council improvement notices in last 6 months
- No unlawful fees/deposits outstanding

**Time Limits:**
- Minimum **2 months' notice** required
- Cannot use if less than 4 months since tenancy started
- Cannot use if required documents not provided

**Filing Location:**
- Served on tenant (not filed with court initially)
- If tenant doesn't leave, apply to court for possession order

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Not providing required documents
- Incorrect notice period
- Using when prohibited (e.g., unlicensed HMO)
- Not protecting deposit
- Wrong form version

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Low-medium complexity
- 2-3 pages
- Must use correct version of form

### 3.2 Section 8 Notice

**Official Form Name:** Section 8 Notice (various forms depending on grounds)

Landlord's notice to evict when tenant breaches tenancy terms (rent arrears, antisocial behaviour, etc.)

- Landlord details
- Tenant details
- Property address
- Grounds for possession (must specify)
- Details of breach
- Date notice served
- Date tenant must leave (varies by ground - 2 weeks to 2 months)

- Valid tenancy agreement
- Breach of tenancy terms
- Should attempt to resolve dispute first (e.g., rent repayment plan)

**Time Limits:**
- **2 weeks** for serious breaches (Ground 8 - rent arrears 2+ months)
- **2 months** for other breaches
- From May 2026: landlord's need to occupy/sell property grounds available

**Filing Location:**
- Served on tenant
- If tenant doesn't leave, apply to court for possession order

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Wrong notice period
- Not specifying correct grounds
- Insufficient detail of breach
- Not attempting resolution first
- Wrong form for grounds

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Medium complexity
- 2-4 pages
- Must use correct form for specific grounds

## 4. Benefits & Social Security

### 4.1 SSCS1 - Social Security Benefits Appeal

**Official Form Name:** Appeal a social security benefits decision (Notice of appeal): Form SSCS1  
**Form ID:** SSCS1  
**Last Updated:** August 2021  
**Official URL:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/appeal-a-social-security-benefits-decision-form-sscs1

**What it's for:**  
Appeal DWP decisions about social security benefits (PIP, ESA, Universal Credit, Jobseeker's Allowance, etc.)

**Key Sections/Structure:**
- Your details (name, address, NI number)
- Benefit decision details
- **Mandatory Reconsideration Notice (MRN)** - must have this first
- Grounds for appeal
- Why you think decision is wrong
- Supporting evidence
- Whether you want a hearing
- Representative details (if any)

- **Mandatory Reconsideration Notice (MRN)** required first (unless decision letter says otherwise)

- **1 month** from date of MRN (mandatory reconsideration notice)
- Can appeal late with good reason (e.g., hospitalization, bereavement)

**Filing Location:**
- Online: www.gov.uk/appeal-benefit-decision (preferred)
- Post: HMCTS Social Security and Child Support Tribunal
- Email: Available for some submissions

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Not having MRN first
- Missing 1-month deadline
- Insufficient grounds
- Not providing evidence
- Not requesting hearing if needed

**Typical Length/Complexity:**
- Medium complexity
- 4-6 pages
- Requires understanding of benefit rules
